JELKA STOJANOV

ABOUT

I am an experimental social psychologist combining behavioral, psychophysiological, and eye-tracking methods to understand how social groups shape and are shaped by emotions.

Since October 2019, I am based at the University of Oxford, where I am enrolled in a postgraduate program DPhil (PhD) in Experimental Psychology and funded by the Dulverton Trust (Dulverton Scholarship) and the Rotary Foundation (Rotary D1090 Global Grant).

Since January 2023, I am based at the University of Birmingham, where I am a Research Fellow in the Autism, Social Cognition, and Bodily Movement lab.
